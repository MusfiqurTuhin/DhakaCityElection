Detailed Demographics & Administration

Demographic Profile (2022 Census)

  • Gender Composition: For the first time, a female-majority was recorded, reflecting the national trend (50.46% F vs 49.54% M). BBS
  • Age Cohort: High urban migration drives a young population, with ~40% aged 18-35. DHS
  • Urban Density (DNCC): 30,531 persons/km². BBS
  • Urban Density (DSCC): ~39,400 persons/km² (Among the world's highest). BBS
  • Migration: Rapid growth from 1971-2001 (5-6% CAGR BBS) has slowed, but migration remains the primary driver of change.
  • Informal Settlements: An estimated 2-3 million people (20-25% of the city) live in informal slums. Media

Ward Boundary Changes

Bifurcation (Nov 29, 2011)

The unified 90-ward DCC was dissolved. LGD It was split into DNCC (initial 54 wards, 82.69 km²) and DSCC (initial 57 wards, ~109 km²). The 2015 election was held on this map.

Expansion (July 29, 2017)

36 new wards were added (18 to each corporation) by absorbing surrounding unions. LGD This fundamentally altered the 2020 voter base.

  • DNCC: Grew by +138% to 197 km². LGD
  • DSCC: Grew by +59% to ~173 km². LGD

Data Limitations & Sources

Data Gaps & Limitations

  • Pre-2015 Ward Data: Ward-level results for 1994 and 2002 are not digitized, preventing granular historical analysis.
  • Campaign Finance: No mandatory, transparent reporting framework. Spending limits (e.g., 5M in DNCC 2015 Media) were widely ignored.
  • Administrative Gaps: Records for appointed administrators, especially during military rule (1982-1986), are sparse.
  • Demographic Links: Detailed ward-level census data from 2022 is not yet public, limiting analysis on turnout predictors.

Primary Data Sources
